Abstract

In rare but safety-critical driving scenarios, we hypothesize that trajectory outcomes become increasingly multi-modal based on differences between driver style compared to non-critical, common scenarios. However, current approaches for trajectory prediction rarely account for differences in driving style, leading to behavior corresponding to "averaged" driving style. While average-case behavior may work well in common scenarios, it limits the diversity of outcomes in rare events or tail-end cases, which may be increasingly stressful for drivers. Extraction of driving style has several benefits, as it enables 1) statistical analysis of driving style occurrence in large driving datasets, 2) simulation of counterfactual outcomes in real-world log replays, and 3) cross-comparison with real-world studies on the relationship between driving style and crash risk. This paper presents a parameter-efficient Mixture-of-Experts framework for extraction of latent driving styles in trajectory prediction models. We choose a parameter-efficient approach to reduce catastrophic forgetting in well-generalized trajectory prediction models, while offering portability of trained driving style modules.
